numberz

numberz is a fintech startup focussed on building products to help businesses manage their cash flow and account receivables. I joined numberz as one of their first designers and led the design of cashflow management - ios app single-handedly. After successfully launching the app, I worked with founders on a few product ideas around GST tax filing & payment collections for Chartered Accountants. By this time I had built a multi-disciplinary design team comprising of talents with complementary competencies: a ux intern, ux researcher-cum-designer & a ux-engg. Together we designed, validated & released Account Receivables web app in early 2018.

Role:  Head of Design

Duration:  2017-18

Cashflow Management for Small Businesses | ios app

The brief was to design a simple invoicing & expense management ios app that helps numberz’s existing web app customers manage their business anytime & anywhere on mobile. We followed ios first approach since the existing user base was mostly iphone users.


I studied the existing web app and sifted through customer complaints to identify the problem areas in the workflows. I got founder's alignment on 'not replicating the web experience on app' since the user expectation and interactions on mobile are different. Thinking mobile forced us to think about what’s really important for customers. As a result, we designed & released the core functionalities first, i.e. letting customer create, send and track invoices & expenses and view critical reports with ease on mobile.

Target customers

The target customers were existing numberz’s cashflow management web app users, consisting of freelancers and small businesses owners.

Make most of the data in a glance

The home screen was designed to give the user an overview on the data-set that needs to be attended in one glance. This screen is split into two parts:


1. Top Carousel: Gives an overview of..

> Outstanding invoices & Money In

> Pending expenses & Money Out

> Money gain or loss: Health of the bank balance

Based on customer validation, the invoice view is kept as default view since most of the customers were keen on seeing the money they need to chase (outstanding invoices) first.


2. Bottom list view: This contains upcoming follow-ups for Invoices or Expenses that needs to be attended.

Designed for speed & simplicity

The flow designed to help merchant create, send, and track invoices, estimates & expenses was kept linear so that they can predict what will happen next. This minimizes the effort required by a user to achieve their goal

Bespoke modular UI

Form, scale and layout of information honours the data be it in the list view (all invoice page) or card views (Invoice detail page). Idea was to create a layout that felt bespoke, with big numbers and generous spacing but didn’t sacrifice modularity

Deliver information in an easier, more consumable way

The key reports were restructured for mobile consumption in a way that they meaningfully communicate KPIs at the same time provide a wealth of data (as shown in web tables) in an easy to read list view format. Incorporated generous spacing between elements in & around bar graph so that the touch targets are easier to tap on.

Account Receivable | web & mobile responsive app

Account receivable is a CRM of payment collection designed to make collecting money fast, easy, and highly predictable. It was initially conceptualised & built as an easy to use invoice presentment tool to help mid-size businesses get a one shot-view of their outstanding: due, overdue invoices they need to chase for payment collection, with the help of a lightweight automated reminder engine. The p.o.c was thoroughly validated in the presale customer interactions both by sales, design & pm team with the help of design prototypes.


Once we got enough validations and decent customer adoption at the mid-size business segment, we designed and upgraded it to full-fledged CRM for collections to fulfill the needs of large businesses with dedicated finance & collection teams struggling with tedious excels in managing their collections.

Target customers

The target customers varried from accountants & sales team (who double up as collection agents) in mid-size busineses (20-100cr) to finance & sales teams in large busineses (100cr & above).

Contributors:  I led a team of 3 Product Designers

An overview of the high lvl structure of the app

A CRM for collections to get businesses paid faster

Problem: One of the big challenges in small, medium or large business is ensuring their customers pay on time so that their business cashflow remains healthy. Customers many times take 15-50 days to pay over the assigned credit period and this puts business cashflow at risk. The existing ERP solutions don't cover workflows around payment collections like keeping a track of business & customer interaction on payments/disputes. Hence, reducing the scope of tracking data that reflects customer’s behavior dynamically, and on a real-time basis.


Solution: We designed CRM for payment collection with integrated task & activity workflows to manage collection at customer & invoice level, tracking every bit of information generated during the interaction and progress in real-time. The solution helps the collection team follow up on prioritized tasks, track expected payments, promise-to-pay & solve payment disputes. It also offers insights on the collection team’s performance and better visibility of high & low-risk customers. Designing for better visibility of information that will aid in timely collection & the workflows around it was the challenge we had.

Designing tables

Designing an enterprise application means one has to deal with tons of data and grids and tables. Efficient data tables allow users to scan, analyze, compare, filter, sort, and manipulate information to derive insights and take informed actions. Some of the fundamental principles that guided the table design process:

1. Ability to scan lots of data at once, i.e., show various status & data types like numerals & text.

2. Help user take informed actions by easy comparison of data in columns & rows.

Designing nu UI style guide

An early investment in designing a basic UI style guide in parallel with product helped significantly in building proof of concepts fast be it at a feature level or a whole new product being conceptualized from grounds up. I didn't have to re-invent the wheel to think about fundamentals like color, use of icons, typography, buttons, etc. This also helped me achieve an almost consistent design language in my workflows, across all products.


As our product scaled, and so did our design team. More no. of UI components were designed, old components like filters, date pickers, tables, etc were improved upon to solve more business use cases. By the time I had one more designer contributing to designing component and another consuming it, hence the need to make our guidelines more mature & accessible so that it's easier to use and clearer to discern both for designers & developers with various backgrounds.

Contributors:  2 Product Designers (incl. myself)

Upgrading basic UI style guide to a more mature guidline doc

Inspired by Brad Frost’s Atomic Design methodology I re-organized our basic UI style guides by segregating UI components into atoms, molecules, organisms & templates, such that ‘the part shapes the whole and the whole shapes the part’ philosophy was more evident. This helped designers while designing a composite component as they would know what all fundamental components they could re-use to build the whole. This re-organization also helped in cleaning & restructuring certain composite components (molecules & organisms) which were detached from already defined UI fundamentals (atoms).
